New York City, New York


New york City is America’s financial and cultural center. It is also the most densely populated city in the US. It is famously called the city that never sleeps because of its 24/7 liveliness. NYC’s traits perfectly represent the United States of America as a whole, a melting pot of peoples and cultures that altogether form the most awe-inspiring place on the planet.

Empire State Building

Of the top five tallest buildings in New York City, the Empire State Building is by far the oldest. Construction of the building began in 1930 and, after an incredible 13 months (just 410 days), was completed in 1931. New York's most iconic building.

Times Square

Times Square is a central plaza and intersection in Midtown Manhattan. It is known for the large commercial billboards on its building and the enormous amounts of traffic it attracts daily. Times Square is also the venue for the annual New Year’s Eve Ball Drop event.

Madison Square Garden

Madison Square Garden is an indoor arena for hosting sports games and concerts. It is the world’s most famous arena and among the most visited yearly.

Museum of Modern Art

The Museum of Modern Art in Midtown Manhattan is dedicated to curating modern and contemporary art. It possesses an extensive collection of artwork and is especially memorable for its modern architectural design.
